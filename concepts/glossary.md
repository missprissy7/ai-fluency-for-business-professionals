# Glossary

A quick reference for terms used throughout this curriculum. Look things up here as you encounter them.

---

**Agent** - An AI system that takes a sequence of actions to complete a multi-step task, rather than just answering a single question. See: concepts/what-is-an-agent.md

**AI (Artificial Intelligence)** - Software that performs tasks that normally require human intelligence, such as understanding language, generating text, and answering questions.

**Claude** - An AI assistant made by Anthropic. Available at claude.ai.

**Context** - All the information the AI has available when generating a response. More relevant context leads to better outputs.

**Context Window** - The maximum amount of text an AI model can process at once. Think of it as the AI's working memory for a conversation.

**ChatGPT** - An AI assistant made by OpenAI. Available at chat.openai.com.

**Hallucination** - When an AI generates information that sounds plausible but is factually incorrect or made up. Always verify important facts.

**Issue (GitHub)** - An assignment in this curriculum. Issues are how your tasks are delivered to you. You complete the work, post your deliverable in a comment, and close the issue.

**Iterate** - To refine an AI response through follow-up instructions rather than accepting the first output. Iteration is how professionals get high-quality AI output.

**LLM (Large Language Model)** - The type of AI that powers most chat tools. Trained on large amounts of text to understand and generate language.

**Model** - The underlying AI system that powers a tool. Different models have different strengths.

**Perplexity** - An AI research tool that searches the web in real time and cites sources. Available at perplexity.ai.

**Prompt** - The instruction or question you give to an AI tool. Prompt quality directly determines output quality.

**RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)** - A technique that lets AI retrieve information from specific documents before generating a response. Used in tools like NotebookLM.

**Repository (Repo)** - A project folder on GitHub containing all the files for this curriculum.

**ROLE + TASK + FORMAT** - The three-part prompt structure taught in Phase 2. Defines who the AI is acting as, what it should do, and how it should present the output.

**Tool Calling** - When an AI connects to an external service or tool to get real-time information or take an action.
