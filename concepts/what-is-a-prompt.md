# What is a Prompt?

**The short answer:** A prompt is the instruction or question you give to an AI tool. The quality of your prompt directly determines the quality of the response you get back.

## Think of It Like a Work Assignment

Imagine you are delegating a task to a highly capable but very literal assistant. If you say "write something about our project," they have no idea what you actually need. If you say "write a one-page executive summary of Project X for our CFO, focused on budget and timeline, in professional but direct language," they can do exactly what you need.

That is the difference between a weak prompt and a strong prompt.

## The Three Parts of a Strong Prompt

**ROLE** - Tell the AI who it is playing:
"You are an experienced operations manager..."

**TASK** - Tell the AI exactly what to do:
"Your job is to write a process improvement recommendation for..."

**FORMAT** - Tell the AI how to present the output:
"Present your response as a one-page memo with headers, under 400 words."

## Why Prompting is a Learnable Skill

You will not write perfect prompts on the first try. Neither does anyone else. The skill is in iterating - reading the response, identifying what is missing or wrong, and refining your instruction. With practice, you will develop instincts for what level of detail a given task requires.

## Common Prompt Mistakes

- Too vague: "Help me with this."
- No format specified: AI will choose its own format, which may not fit your need
- No audience specified: The tone will be generic
- No length specified: AI may write much more or less than you need
- Asking multiple unrelated things at once: Break complex tasks into steps
