# What is an LLM?

**The short answer:** LLM stands for Large Language Model. It is the type of AI that powers ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, and most of the AI tools you are using in this curriculum.

## How It Works (Simply)

An LLM is trained by processing enormous amounts of text - books, websites, articles, code, conversations - and learning statistical patterns about which words and ideas follow each other. When you give it a prompt, it uses those patterns to generate a response that is likely to be relevant and coherent.

It is not retrieving a stored answer from a database. It is generating new text based on learned patterns. This is why AI can be creative and flexible - and also why it can be wrong.

## Why This Matters Practically

- LLMs do not have memory between sessions by default
- LLMs have a training cutoff date - they may not know about recent events
- LLMs are good at language tasks, not math or real-time data
- The same LLM can behave differently depending on how you prompt it

## Large vs. Small Models

LLMs come in different sizes. Larger models generally produce better results on complex tasks but are slower and more expensive to run. The free tiers of the tools in this curriculum use capable models that are more than sufficient for business use.

## What You Do Not Need to Know

You do not need to understand neural networks, training data, or model architecture to use AI effectively. What you need is good prompting skills and the judgment to evaluate outputs - both of which this curriculum teaches.
