# What is RAG?

**The short answer:** RAG stands for Retrieval-Augmented Generation. It is a technique that lets AI tools look up information from specific documents or databases before generating a response, rather than relying only on what they learned during training.

## Why This Matters to You

Standard AI tools are limited to what they learned before their training cutoff. RAG fixes this by connecting the AI to a specific set of documents - your company files, a knowledge base, a set of reports - so it can answer questions based on your actual content.

## A Practical Example

Without RAG: You ask an AI to summarize your company's Q3 report. It cannot - it has never seen that document.

With RAG: You upload your Q3 report to a RAG-enabled tool (like NotebookLM). Now the AI can answer questions about it, summarize specific sections, or compare it to your Q2 report.

## Tools That Use RAG

- **NotebookLM** (Google) - Upload your own documents and chat with them
- **Perplexity** - Retrieves from the live web before answering
- Many enterprise AI tools built on top of company databases

## The Key Insight

RAG is what makes AI useful for your specific organizational knowledge. General AI tools know about the world. RAG-enabled tools can know about your world. As AI tools mature, RAG capabilities will become standard - being familiar with the concept now prepares you for more powerful tools ahead.
