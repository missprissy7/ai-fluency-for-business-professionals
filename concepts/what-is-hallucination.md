# What is Hallucination?

**The short answer:** Hallucination is when an AI generates information that sounds plausible and confident but is factually incorrect or entirely made up.

## Why It Happens

AI language models work by predicting what text should come next based on patterns learned during training. They are optimized to produce fluent, coherent-sounding text. Sometimes that fluency leads the model to generate something that sounds right but is not - including fake statistics, incorrect dates, non-existent citations, or wrong names.

Importantly, the AI does not know it is wrong. It has no internal fact-checking mechanism. It generates text that fits the pattern of a correct answer even when the content is false.

## Examples of Common Hallucinations

- Citing a study or statistic that does not exist
- Getting names, titles, or dates wrong
- Describing a process incorrectly while sounding authoritative
- Making up a company, product, or person
- Confidently answering a question it does not actually have data for

## How to Protect Yourself

1. **Verify facts that matter.** If a number, name, date, or citation is important, check it independently.
2. **Be especially careful with:** statistics, legal or regulatory information, medical information, and anything involving specific people or organizations.
3. **Use Perplexity for research.** Because Perplexity cites sources from the live web, it is easier to verify where information came from.
4. **Ask the AI how confident it is.** Prompt: "How confident are you in this answer? Are there any parts you are less certain about?"
5. **Trust your expertise.** If something sounds off in your area of knowledge, it probably is.

## The Bottom Line

Hallucination does not mean AI is useless. It means AI requires a skilled reviewer - which is you. Your professional judgment is not replaced by AI. It is the quality control layer that makes AI output trustworthy.
