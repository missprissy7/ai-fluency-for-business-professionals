# What is Context?

**The short answer:** Context is all the information the AI has available when generating a response. The more relevant context you provide, the better the output.

## Why Context Matters

AI tools do not know anything about you, your organization, your clients, or your work unless you tell them. Every time you start a new conversation, you are starting fresh. This is different from working with a human colleague who already knows your history.

## Types of Context to Provide

**Role context:** Who you are and what you do
"I am a project manager at a mid-size healthcare organization..."

**Audience context:** Who will read or use the output
"This is for a non-technical executive who needs a quick summary..."

**Situation context:** What is happening and why this task exists
"We are preparing for a client renewal meeting next week..."

**Constraint context:** What limits or requirements apply
"Keep it under one page. Do not mention pricing. Use formal language."

**Example context:** A sample of what good looks like
"Here is an example of the format I want: [paste example]"

## The Context Window

Every AI model has a limit on how much text it can process at once - this is called the context window. For practical purposes, you can think of it as the AI's working memory for your current conversation. Very long conversations or very large documents may exceed this limit. If an AI seems to forget something you mentioned earlier, this may be why.

## Practical Tip

For recurring tasks, build your standard context into your prompt template. That way you do not have to re-explain your situation every time.
