# 🚀 Getting Started
## Your Complete Setup Guide — From Zero to Ready

> Take your time with this. A good setup means smooth learning. A rushed setup means frustration later.

---

## ✅ Before You Begin — Checklist

- [ ] A computer (Windows, Mac, or Linux)
- [ ] Reliable internet connection
- [ ] An email address you can access
- [ ] 30–60 minutes to complete this setup

That's it. You don't need any technical experience.

---

## Step 1: Create a GitHub Account (10 minutes)

GitHub is where this entire curriculum lives. Think of it as a shared workspace where your assignments (Issues) are waiting for you and where you'll post your completed work.

1. Go to **https://github.com/signup**
2. Enter your email address and click **Continue**
3. Create a password (write it down somewhere safe)
4. Choose a username — this is public, so keep it professional (e.g., `firstname-lastname` or your initials)
5. Complete the verification puzzle
6. Check your email and click the verification link GitHub sends you
7. On the "Welcome" screen, you can skip the optional questions — scroll down and click **Skip personalization**

✅ **Checkpoint:** You can log into https://github.com with your new account.

---

## Step 2: Install the GitHub Mobile App (5 minutes)

The mobile app lets you check your issues, post comments, and track your progress from your phone.

1. Open the **App Store** (iPhone) or **Google Play Store** (Android)
2. Search for **GitHub**
3. Install the official app by GitHub, Inc. (black octocat logo)
4. Open the app and sign in with your new GitHub account

✅ **Checkpoint:** You can see your GitHub profile in the mobile app.

## Step 5: Understand GitHub Issues (5 minutes)

GitHub Issues are your assignments. Here's how to use them:

**To find your issues:**
1. Go to your repository
2. Click the **Issues** tab at the top
3. You'll see a list of open issues — these are your tasks
4. Issues assigned to you will show your profile picture

**To work an issue:**
1. Click on an issue to open it
2. Read the full description — don't skip anything
3. Complete the exercises
4. Scroll to the bottom and click **Write a comment**
5. Post your deliverable (what you made or observed)
6. Click **Close issue** when complete (there's a button at the bottom)

> 💡 **Closing an issue is your "I'm done."** Don't close it until you've posted your deliverable.

✅ **Checkpoint:** You can navigate to an issue, read it, and understand how to post a comment.

## 🆘 If You Get Stuck

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Can't create a GitHub account | Try a different browser, or use incognito mode |
| GitHub invitation email didn't arrive | Check spam folder; ask your instructor to resend |
| AI tool won't let me sign up | Try a different tool — all three do the same thing |
| Don't understand a term | Check the [Concepts](concepts/) folder or the [Glossary](concepts/glossary.md) |
| An exercise doesn't make sense | Re-read it slowly; if still unclear, post a comment on the issue asking for clarification |
| Falling behind schedule | That's okay — this is self-paced. Focus on quality over speed. |
