# Phase 4: Mastery (Week 4)

> **Goal:** Move from individual tasks to designed workflows. Leave with a personal AI system that compounds over time.

## Overview

Phases 1-3 taught you to use AI on individual tasks. Phase 4 is about stepping back and designing how AI fits into your work as a whole.

**By the end of Phase 4, you will:**
- Map a full business process and identify where AI belongs
- Know at least one specialized AI tool beyond the general assistants
- Have a polished prompt library ready to use and share
- Complete a capstone deliverable that serves as your portfolio artifact

## Hands-On Activities

### Activity 4.1: Process Mapping with AI (60 minutes)

**Objective:** Map a complete business process and annotate where AI can own steps.

**Steps:**

1. Pick a complete end-to-end business process you own or participate in
   Examples: client onboarding, monthly reporting, proposal development, new hire orientation

2. Map it manually first - list every step from start to finish

3. For each step, label it:
   - AI CAN OWN: AI can do this with minimal oversight
   - AI ASSISTS: AI helps, but a human reviews and decides
   - HUMAN ONLY: Requires human judgment, relationship, or accountability

4. For every AI step, identify:
   - Which tool you would use
   - What the prompt structure would look like
   - How you would verify the output

5. Calculate: What percentage of this process could be AI-assisted?

**Reflection Questions:**
- What surprised you about where AI can and cannot help?
- What would this process look like in 2 years if AI tools continue to improve?
- What is the risk if AI makes a mistake at a critical step?

**Deliverable:** Post in the issue comment: Your process name, number of steps, and the percentage you categorized as AI-assisted. What is the highest-value AI opportunity in this process?

### Activity 4.2: Explore a Specialized Tool (60 minutes)

**Objective:** Go beyond general AI assistants and explore a tool built for a specific job.

General AI tools are generalists. Specialized tools are built for specific tasks and often produce better results in their lane.

**Choose one tool to explore:**

| Tool | Best For | Link |
|------|----------|------|
| Perplexity (deep research mode) | Research with cited sources | https://perplexity.ai |
| NotebookLM by Google | Chatting with your own documents | https://notebooklm.google.com |
| Gamma | Turning content into presentations | https://gamma.app |
| Otter.ai | Meeting transcription and summaries | https://otter.ai |

**Steps:**

1. Choose one tool based on what matters most in your work
2. Sign up for a free account if needed
3. Complete one real task using the tool
4. Compare: How does this tool's output compare to ChatGPT or Claude on the same task?

**Reflection Questions:**
- What did this specialized tool do that a general AI could not match?
- Where did it fall short?
- Is this a tool you would add to your regular workflow?

**Deliverable:** Post in the issue comment: Which tool you chose and one specific way it was better or worse than a general AI assistant.

### Activity 4.3: Polish Your Prompt Library (60 minutes)

**Objective:** Refine and finalize your prompt library so it is production-ready.

**Steps:**

1. Open notes/my-prompt-library.md from Phase 2
2. Review each template - test any that have not been tested
3. Add at least 3 new templates based on what you learned in Phases 3 and 4
4. Add a Quick Reference table at the top listing all templates by name and use case
5. Ask yourself: Could I share this with a colleague today and have them use it without explanation?

**Reflection Questions:**
- What makes a prompt template truly reusable vs. situational?
- What is missing from your library that you know you will need?
- How would you keep this library updated as AI tools evolve?

**Deliverable:** Post in the issue comment: How many templates are in your library now? Paste your Quick Reference table.

### Activity 4.4: Capstone Project (2-3 hours)

**Objective:** Demonstrate everything you have learned by completing a substantial real work deliverable entirely AI-assisted and documenting the process.

The capstone is not a test. It is a production. You are creating something real and documenting how you made it.

**Steps:**

1. Choose your capstone deliverable. It must be:
   - Something you would actually use in your work
   - Substantial enough to require multiple AI interactions
   - Something a colleague would recognize as professional quality

   Examples:
   - A complete project proposal or business case
   - A multi-section operational report
   - A training document or process guide for your team
   - A client-facing presentation outline with talking points
   - A strategic plan for a process improvement initiative

2. Define success before you start:
   - Who is this for?
   - What does the final version need to contain?
   - What would make this excellent vs. just acceptable?

3. Complete the deliverable using AI, applying everything from Phases 1-4:
   - Use ROLE + TASK + FORMAT
   - Iterate at least 5 times
   - Ask AI to critique its own output
   - Use multiple tools if helpful
   - Verify all factual claims

4. Document your process in notes/capstone-process.md:

   Capstone: [Deliverable Name]
   What I Built: [Description]
   Tools Used: [Which AI tools and what each was used for]
   Key Prompts: [The 2-3 most important prompts]
   What AI Did Well: [Specific examples]
   What AI Got Wrong: [Specific examples and how you corrected them]
   Time Comparison: [Estimated time with AI vs. without]
   What I Would Do Differently: [Honest reflection]

5. Final quality check:
   - Would you submit this to your most demanding client or stakeholder?
   - Is there anything in it you cannot verify?
   - Does it represent your professional standard?

**Deliverable:** Post in the issue comment: Paste your capstone deliverable AND your process document. This is your portfolio artifact.
