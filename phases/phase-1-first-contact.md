# Phase 1: First Contact (Week 1)

> **Goal:** Eliminate the blank-cursor problem. Get comfortable talking to AI and produce your first real AI-assisted work product.

## Overview

Most people freeze when they open an AI tool for the first time. By the end of this week, that feeling will be gone.

**By the end of Phase 1, you will:**
- Open an AI tool and have a productive conversation without hesitation
- Understand the difference between a vague request and a specific one
- Recognize when AI gives you a bad answer
- Produce at least one piece of work you would actually use

## Hands-On Activities

### Activity 1.1: Break the Ice (30 minutes)

**Objective:** Get comfortable opening an AI tool and having a conversation.

**Steps:**

1. Open Perplexity AI at https://perplexity.ai
2. Type the following and press Enter:
   "I am a business professional just learning to use AI tools. What are three things I should know about working with AI that most beginner tutorials skip?"
3. Read the response carefully
4. Ask a follow-up question based on something in the answer
5. Continue the conversation for at least 5 exchanges
6. Open Claude at https://claude.ai and ask the SAME opening question
7. Compare the two responses

**Reflection Questions:**
- Which response felt more useful? Why?
- What surprised you about one or both answers?
- What does keeping the conversation going feel like vs. starting fresh each time?

**Deliverable:** Post in the issue comment: Two things you noticed that were different between the two tools' responses.

---

### Activity 1.2: Vague vs. Specific (45 minutes)

**Objective:** Learn how the quality of your question determines the quality of the answer.

**Steps:**

1. Pick a topic relevant to your work
2. Ask AI about it in three ways:

   **Version 1 - Vague:** "Help me with my work."

   **Version 2 - Specific:** "Help me write a meeting agenda for a 30-minute weekly status meeting with my team."

   **Version 3 - Ultra-Specific:** "Help me write a 30-minute weekly status meeting agenda for a team of 5 people who manage client accounts. Include time for blockers, upcoming deadlines, and one win from last week. Format it as a numbered list with time allocations."

3. Save all three responses and compare them side by side

**Reflection Questions:**
- What did the vague version give you? Was it usable?
- What changed when you got specific?
- What does this tell you about how to approach AI going forward?

**Deliverable:** Post in the issue comment: Paste your Version 3 prompt and the response. Was it usable as-is, or did you still need to edit it?

---

### Activity 1.3: Catch the AI Being Wrong (45 minutes)

**Objective:** Learn to evaluate AI output critically rather than accept it at face value.

**Steps:**

1. Ask AI a question about something you actually know well:
   "Explain [your topic] to someone who has never heard of it."
2. Read the response carefully
3. Find at least one thing that is inaccurate, oversimplified, or missing
4. Point it out to the AI and ask it to revise
5. Repeat once more if you find another issue

**Reflection Questions:**
- Was it easy or hard to find the error?
- How did the AI respond when you corrected it?
- What would have happened if you had just accepted the first response?

**Deliverable:** Post in the issue comment: Describe what was wrong in the AI's first response and how you corrected it.

**Important:** AI tools can generate confident-sounding incorrect information. This is called hallucination. Critical evaluation is not optional - it is a core skill.

---

### Activity 1.4: Your First Real Work Product (60-90 minutes)

**Objective:** Use AI to produce something you would actually use at work.

**Steps:**

1. Choose a real work task:
   - A professional email you have been putting off writing
   - An agenda for an upcoming meeting
   - A summary of a document you need to share
   - A first draft of a report or proposal section
   - Talking points for a conversation you need to have

2. Before prompting AI, write down:
   - Who is the audience?
   - What is the purpose?
   - What format should the output be?
   - What constraints apply?

3. Build your prompt and ask the AI
4. Review the output
5. Iterate at least twice
6. Produce a final version you are satisfied with

**Reflection Questions:**
- How long did this take compared to doing it without AI?
- What did AI do well? What did it get wrong?
- What would you do differently next time?

**Deliverable:** Post in the issue comment: Describe the task you chose and paste your final version or a meaningful excerpt. Did it save you time?
