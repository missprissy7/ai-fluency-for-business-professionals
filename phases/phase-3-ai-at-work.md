# Phase 3: AI at Work (Week 3)

> **Goal:** Apply AI directly to your real work. Every activity this week produces something you would actually use.

## Overview

Phases 1 and 2 built your foundation. Phase 3 is where it becomes real. Every exercise this week is grounded in your actual job.

**By the end of Phase 3, you will:**
- Identify which of your work tasks are best suited for AI assistance
- Complete at least one end-to-end work product using AI
- Use AI to prepare for a real upcoming situation
- Build a reusable workflow for a task you repeat regularly

## Hands-On Activities

### Activity 3.1: The AI Task Audit (45 minutes)

**Objective:** Identify where AI can and cannot help in your actual work.

Not all tasks are equal. Some are perfect for AI; others should stay manual. Knowing the difference is a professional skill.

**Steps:**

1. List your 10 most time-consuming or repetitive work tasks

2. Rate each task using this scoring guide:

| Dimension | Score 1 | Score 2 | Score 3 |
|-----------|---------|---------|--------|
| Repeatability | Happens rarely | Happens monthly | Happens weekly/daily |
| Writing-heavy | Mostly numbers/data | Mix | Mostly words |
| Verifiable | Hard to check output | Somewhat checkable | Easy to verify |
| Sensitive | High stakes/confidential | Medium | Low stakes |

3. Total each task's score. High scores = good AI candidates.
4. Pick your top 3 AI-suitable tasks for the rest of Phase 3

**Reflection Questions:**
- What surprised you about which tasks scored high?
- Which tasks should never be AI-assisted? Why?
- How does this audit change how you will plan your work week?

**Deliverable:** Post in the issue comment: Your top 3 AI-suitable tasks with a one-sentence explanation of why each made the list.

### Activity 3.2: End-to-End Work Product (90 minutes)

**Objective:** Complete a real, full work product from start to finish using AI.

**Steps:**

1. Choose one of your top 3 tasks from Activity 3.1
2. Define the deliverable completely before you start:
   - Who is it for?
   - What is its purpose?
   - What format should it be in?
   - What does done look like?
3. Build your ROLE + TASK + FORMAT prompt
4. Generate the first draft
5. Iterate at least 3 rounds of revision
6. Do a final quality check:
   - Is anything factually wrong?
   - Does the tone fit the audience?
   - Would you submit this as-is?
7. Finalize it

**Reflection Questions:**
- How long did this take compared to doing it without AI?
- What percentage of the final product came from AI vs. your own edits?
- What would you tell a colleague who asked if AI helped you with this?

**Deliverable:** Post in the issue comment: Paste the final work product or a meaningful excerpt. How long did this take with AI vs. without?

### Activity 3.3: AI-Assisted Preparation (60 minutes)

**Objective:** Use AI to prepare for a real upcoming situation.

Professional AI use is not just about creating content. It is about thinking better. Use AI as a preparation partner.

**Steps:**

1. Choose an upcoming situation you need to prepare for:
   - A meeting where you need to make a decision
   - A difficult conversation with a client or stakeholder
   - A presentation you are giving
   - A proposal you are evaluating

2. Ask AI to help you prepare:
   "You are a senior advisor helping a [your role] prepare for [the situation]. Help me by: (1) identifying the 5 most important questions I should be ready to answer, (2) listing potential objections and how to address them, (3) suggesting the 3 most important points to make."

3. Use the output to build your actual preparation notes
4. After the situation happens, reflect on whether the preparation was useful

**Reflection Questions:**
- Did AI surface anything you had not thought of?
- Which part of the preparation was most valuable?
- Would you use AI this way again before important situations?

**Deliverable:** Post in the issue comment: What situation did you prepare for? What was the most useful thing AI helped you anticipate?

---

### Activity 3.4: Build a Repeatable Workflow (45 minutes)

**Objective:** Turn a one-time AI task into a documented, repeatable process.

The goal of AI fluency is not to use AI once. It is to build systems that compound.

**Steps:**

1. Take the work product from Activity 3.2
2. Document the process as a reusable workflow in notes/workflows.md:

   Workflow Name: [Task Name]
   When to Use: [What situation triggers this?]
   Inputs Needed: [What information do you need before starting?]
   Step 1: [Action and instructions]
   Step 2: [Action and instructions]
   Prompt Template: [Your tested ROLE + TASK + FORMAT prompt]
   Quality Checklist:
   - Factually accurate
   - Appropriate tone
   - Correct format
   - Ready to use without major editing
   Time Estimate: [How long does this take with AI?]

3. Test it on a second instance of the same task to verify it works

**Reflection Questions:**
- Could someone else follow this workflow and get a similar result?
- What would you add or change after testing it a second time?
- How many other tasks in your job could have a documented workflow like this?

**Deliverable:** Post in the issue comment: Paste your workflow document.
