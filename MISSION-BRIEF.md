## The One Thing You Need to Know Before Starting

Every AI course you've seen starts the same way: "AI stands for Artificial Intelligence..." and then 40 slides about neural networks.

**This is not that.**

This curriculum starts with a task. You open an issue, read what you need to do, and do it. The learning happens in the doing.

---

## Who This Is For

This program is built for the business professional who:

- Manages projects, teams, processes, or clients
- Has strong instincts about quality, communication, and getting things done
- Knows AI is important but hasn't figured out how to make it actually useful at work
- Learns best by doing real things, not watching demonstrations

**Your existing skills are your biggest advantage.** The ability to define a clear goal, break it into steps, delegate work, and verify the output — that's exactly how you work with AI. You're not starting from zero. You're translating skills you already have into a new domain.

## What You WILL Be Able to Do

By the end of 4 weeks, you will be able to:

1. **Have a productive conversation with any AI tool** — no blank cursor, no wasted time
2. **Write prompts that consistently get useful results** — using a repeatable structure you own
3. **Use AI to complete real work tasks** — drafts, summaries, research, planning, analysis
4. **Evaluate AI output critically** — know when to trust it, when to verify it, and when to reject it
5. **Build reusable AI workflows** — a personal system that compounds over time

## How the Program Works

You have been given access to a GitHub repository. Your curriculum is delivered as **GitHub Issues** — tasks assigned directly to you.

Each issue follows the same format:
- 🎯 A clear learning objective
- ✅ Success criteria with checkboxes
- 🎯 Practical exercises with step-by-step instructions
- 💭 Reflection questions
- 💾 A deliverable you post as a comment before closing the issue

**The rhythm:**
1. Open your assigned issue
2. Read the objective and exercises
3. Complete the work
4. Post your deliverable in a comment
5. Close the issue
6. Open the next one
