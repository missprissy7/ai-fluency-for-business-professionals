Before AI

Diamonds: rare, expensive, valuable

After AI

Diamonds: everywhere, cheap, ordinary

"If diamonds suddenly became like sand — available to everyone — no one would pay a lot for diamonds anymore."

This is not a metaphor about technology. It is a statement about economic value. AI has collapsed the cost of producing things that used to require significant time, skill, or money.

The implication

What does this mean for your business?

Any part of your business built on the scarcity of a skill or the cost of a task is now at risk.

⚠️

Pricing Risk

You're charging based on effort that AI now eliminates. Clients will notice.

⚠️

Workflow Risk

Your processes were designed around human speed. They're now artificially slow.

✦

Opportunity

Organizations that adapt get to do more, serve more, and compete differently.

Not eventually — now. Your competitors are already making this decision.

Objection 01

"What about
hallucinations?"

Yes. AI makes things up. Here's how to think about it.

01

First draft, not final

Every AI output is a starting point. Professional judgment shifts from generation to verification.

02

Use domain-specific tools

Nuance DAX is trained on millions of clinical encounters. Match the tool to the task.

03

Structure reduces risk

Give AI a template to fill, a source to summarize, or a format to follow. Open-ended generation is where risk is highest.

04

This isn't new

Junior employees make things up. Consultants pad reports. The difference is speed. Your job is to build review into the workflow.

💬 Facilitator prompt: "How many of you have reviewed a document written by a human and found errors? Same workflow. AI just drafts faster."

Process — The human side

Where resistance lives

People don't resist AI tools. They resist change to their routines, roles, and sense of competence.

Common pushback — and what it really means

"Our org would never allow this." → There's a security concern. Walk through the tier framework.
"We tried it and it was wrong." → Wrong tool for the task. Let's look at what they used it for.
"This will eliminate jobs." → It eliminates tasks within jobs. The risk is being the org that didn't adapt.
"We're too regulated." → Regulated industries have the most to gain. Show them HIPAA-eligible options.

What actually moves people

  • Communicate the why before the what
  • Involve people in the workflow redesign
  • Name the fear directly — don't minimize it
  • Celebrate early wins publicly
  • Leadership has to model it first

Your action plan

The next 30 days

Week 1

Audit your tools

List every tool your team uses. For each one, ask: is there an AI-infused version?

Week 2

Find one insertion point

What task does your team do every week that feels like a chore? That's your first AI pilot.

Week 3

Give people 2 hours

Block protected time. Not a deliverable — just exploration. See what they discover.

Week 4

Map one process

Take one workflow and draw it. Mark every step AI could do. Redesign it.
