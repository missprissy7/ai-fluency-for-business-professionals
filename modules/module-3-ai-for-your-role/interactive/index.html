A · 01

Healthcare Operations

Patient Flow & No-Show Reduction

The pain: An 18% no-show rate costs a 12-provider practice $200K–$400K in annual lost revenue. The fix is consistent, personalized communication — which no one has time to write for every patient.

The prompt

"I run a 12-provider outpatient family medicine practice. Our no-show rate is 18%, highest on Monday mornings and Friday afternoons. Draft a 3-part patient reminder sequence (72 hours, 24 hours, 2 hours before appointment) that is warm, specific, and includes an easy reschedule path. Also suggest 3 scheduling policy changes based on best practices to reduce no-shows."

What you get

  • 3 ready-to-deploy patient reminder templates
  • Evidence-based scheduling policy recommendations
  • Customizable for SMS, email, or phone script
Before: 3–4 hrs to draft + review → After: 8 min
Tools: ChatGPT GPT-4o Claude
A · 02

Healthcare Operations

Department Reporting & Executive Communication

The pain: The same metrics get repackaged into leadership summaries every month. The data changes; the structure and the writing effort don't.

The prompt

"Write a 3-paragraph executive summary for hospital leadership using these metrics: ED wait time avg 47 min (target 40), patient satisfaction 82% (target 85%), supply costs $1.2M (budget $1.1M), staff overtime 12% (target 8%). Explain variances, identify root causes where I specify them, outline 2–3 corrective actions. Tone: direct, data-driven, not defensive."
Follow-up: "Rewrite the supply cost section — add that Q1 had an unplanned sterile supply backorder requiring spot purchases."

What you get

  • Submit-ready executive summary
  • Variance explanations in leadership language
  • Corrective action framing that doesn't sound defensive
Before: 45–60 min → After: 5 min
Tools: ChatGPT GPT-4o Claude
A · 03

Healthcare Operations

Vendor Performance & Contract Communication

The pain: Vendor performance issues go undocumented because writing the letter takes too long. By the time it gets written, the relationship has deteriorated further.

The prompt

"Draft a professional letter to our medical supply vendor. Their on-time delivery rate dropped to 71% (contract requires 95%). Include: specific data points, operational impact (2 procedure delays, 1 supply substitution incident), a formal 30-day cure period request, and consequences if performance doesn't improve. Tone: firm but professional, not adversarial."

What you get

  • Vendor performance letter ready for legal review
  • Creates a documented paper trail
  • Professional tone that protects the relationship while holding the vendor accountable
Before: 1–2 hrs + legal review → After: 3 min to draft
Key insight: AI doesn't replace legal review — it eliminates the drafting time so legal only reviews, not writes.
A · 04

Healthcare Operations

SOP Building via AI Interview

The pain: Institutional knowledge lives in people's heads. SOPs get written once and never updated. When the person who knows the process leaves, the process breaks.

The prompt

"Interview me and create an SOP for our patient discharge process. Ask me questions one at a time about each step, who is responsible, documentation required, and what can go wrong. After I answer all questions, write the full SOP: title, purpose, scope, responsible parties, step-by-step procedure, documentation requirements, and revision history."

Why this approach works

AI as an interviewer extracts institutional knowledge from the person who actually does the work — not a generic template that gets adapted into something wrong.

The person being interviewed doesn't need to be a writer. They just need to answer questions. The SOP writes itself from the conversation.

Variation: Use this for any process you want documented: credentialing, billing, discharge, scheduling, onboarding.
A · 05

Healthcare Operations

Quality & Patient Safety Documentation

The pain: Incident reports get written under pressure, by people who aren't trained writers, with legal exposure on every word.

The prompt

"Write a structured incident report for a medication near-miss. Details: nurse prepared heparin 10,000 units instead of 1,000 units due to look-alike packaging. Error caught by second nurse during two-person verification. No patient harm. Write in SBAR format and include 3 immediate corrective actions and 2 systemic recommendations."

What you get

  • Complete SBAR incident report
  • Immediate and systemic corrective actions included
  • Quality committee-ready documentation
  • Consistent format regardless of who's writing it

B · 01

Staffing & Workforce

Full-Cycle Recruitment Content

The pain: Every open role needs a job description, an Indeed posting, recruiter outreach copy, and social content. Multiply that by 20 open roles and it's a full-time job just to write the posts.

The prompt

"Create a recruitment content package for a Travel ICU RN opening at a Level II Trauma Center in Atlanta, GA. Include: (1) detailed job description, (2) 150-word Indeed/LinkedIn posting, (3) 3-sentence recruiter outreach for cold LinkedIn reach, (4) two social captions — one LinkedIn, one Facebook. Contract: 13 weeks, $2,800/week all-in, housing stipend. Start May 1."

What you get

  • 4 pieces of channel-specific recruitment content
  • Tone calibrated per platform
  • Ready to post — no copywriter needed
Before: 2+ hrs per role → After: 4 min
Scale it: With 20 open roles, this saves 40+ hours of recruitment content writing per month.
B · 02

Staffing & Workforce

Structured Screening & Interview Guides

The pain: Interview quality is inconsistent. Different managers ask different questions. Scoring is subjective. The result is a hiring process that can't be defended.

The prompt

"I'm hiring a Director of Nursing at a 120-bed SNF. Ideal candidate: 5+ yrs SNF leadership, survey readiness, staff retention record, MDS knowledge. Create: (1) 10-question resume screening checklist with red flags and green flags per criterion, (2) structured interview guide with 8 behavioral questions tied to each competency, (3) a 1–5 scoring rubric for each question. Format as a hiring manager's tool."

What you get

  • Consistent, defensible screening criteria
  • Behavioral questions that surface real evidence
  • Scoring rubric that removes subjectivity
  • Legally cleaner hiring process
Tools: ChatGPT GPT-4o Claude
B · 03

Staffing & Workforce

Onboarding Plans & Welcome Materials

The pain: Every new hire gets a slightly different onboarding experience. The quality of the first 30 days depends entirely on who's managing it.

The prompt

"Create a 30-60-90 day onboarding plan for a newly hired Clinical Nurse Manager on a 28-bed med-surg unit. For each phase: learning objectives, key relationships to build, operational responsibilities to assume, and a success metric. Also write the pre-start welcome letter — warm, role-specific, focused on setting them up to succeed."

What you get

  • Structured 90-day onboarding roadmap
  • Success metrics per phase — not just tasks
  • Welcome letter that starts the relationship right
  • Consistent experience regardless of manager
Variation: Build one template per role family — RN, manager, coordinator, CNA — and customize per hire in minutes.
B · 04

Staffing & Workforce

Credentialing Communication System

The pain: Expired licenses and lapsed certifications are a compliance crisis waiting to happen. Most organizations catch them too late because the reminder system is manual and inconsistent.

The prompt

"Create a credentialing compliance communication system for a home health agency with 45 clinical staff. Design: (1) 90-day advance notice email template for expiring credentials, (2) 30-day final notice with escalation, (3) manager notification template for credentials expiring within 14 days, (4) staff acknowledgment form. All with deadline date placeholders."

What you get

  • Complete 4-piece credentialing communication system
  • Automated-ready — drop into your HR system
  • Manager escalation path built in
  • Paper trail via acknowledgment form
Before: Days to build, ad-hoc execution → After: 10 min to build, systematic execution
B · 05

Staffing & Workforce

Stay Interviews & Retention Conversations

The pain: Healthcare turnover is 19–26% annually. Most managers know who might leave — but don't have the conversation because they don't know how to structure it.

The prompt

"I'm a nursing unit manager preparing stay interviews with 6 nurses at varying flight risk levels. Create: (1) stay interview guide with 8 open-ended questions uncovering what's keeping them and what might make them leave, (2) one-page manager prep sheet for before, during, and after, (3) follow-up action template to document commitments made. Tone: genuinely caring, not corporate HR-speak."

What you get

  • Structured conversation guide managers actually use
  • Questions that uncover real retention drivers
  • Commitment documentation for accountability
  • Consistent approach across all managers

C · 01

Compliance & Risk

AI Governance Policy Development

The pain: Your staff is already using AI tools. Most organizations have no policy governing how. That's a HIPAA incident waiting to happen.

The prompt

"Draft a policy for AI tool use by clinical and administrative staff in a HIPAA-covered organization. Address: (1) approved tool tiers, (2) data classification — what can/cannot be entered into each tier, (3) staff training requirements, (4) incident reporting for AI-related privacy events, (5) governance and annual review. Format: standard P&P with purpose, scope, policy statement, procedures, definitions, references."

Why this matters right now

This is the #1 most-needed document in healthcare compliance in 2026. Most organizations have no AI governance policy. Your staff is using ChatGPT free tier. PHI is being entered into consumer tools. This policy closes that gap.

Tools: Claude ChatGPT Enterprise
C · 02

Compliance & Risk

Audit Preparation & Survey Readiness

The pain: Audit prep is always a fire drill. Documents get pulled, binders get built, staff get coached — all in the last 72 hours, every single time.

The prompt

"Joint Commission survey in 6 weeks. I manage infection prevention. Create: (1) 30-item pre-survey readiness checklist by the top 10 most common Joint Commission IP findings, (2) documentation required for each finding category, (3) one-page staff briefing on what to expect during the survey and how to respond to surveyor questions. Keep it practical — this goes to front-line nursing staff."

What you get

  • Readiness checklist organized by high-risk finding categories
  • Documentation inventory per category
  • Staff briefing in plain language
  • Audit prep that starts weeks earlier, not days
Pattern shift: Audit readiness becomes continuous, not a fire drill. Run this prompt quarterly.
C · 03

Compliance & Risk

HIPAA Breach Documentation & Response

The pain: When a privacy incident happens, documentation needs to be done fast, correctly, and in a way that protects the organization — under pressure, with legal exposure.

The prompt

"Help document a potential HIPAA breach: staff member sent a patient's lab results to the wrong patient's MyChart. Discovered 4 hours later. No highly sensitive info (mental health, substance abuse, HIV). Draft: (1) initial incident documentation using the 4-factor risk assessment, (2) determination memo on breach notification threshold, (3) patient notification letter if required."

What you get

  • Structured 4-factor risk assessment
  • Breach vs. no-breach determination memo
  • OCR-compliant patient notification letter
  • Documentation that protects the organization
Critical note: Always have legal and privacy counsel review before sending breach notifications. AI drafts — counsel decides.
C · 04

Compliance & Risk

Training Content That Doesn't Collect Dust

The pain: Vendor-purchased compliance training is expensive, generic, and immediately stale. Internal development takes weeks. Most of it gets clicked through without being read.

The prompt

"Create a 20-minute HIPAA refresher for front-desk staff. Include: (1) 3 real-world scenarios showing correct vs. incorrect handling of patient info at check-in — make them feel real, not textbook, (2) 10-question quiz with answer key and rationale, (3) 5 key takeaways formatted as a wallet card, (4) a sign-off sheet. Include the awkward moments staff actually face — family in the room, phone calls claiming to be the doctor, wrong fax numbers."

What you get

  • Complete training module — LMS-ready
  • Scenario-based, not lecture-based
  • Quiz with rationale — not just right/wrong
  • Wallet card staff will actually keep
Before: Vendor cost $5K–$20K/yr or weeks of internal dev → After: 15 min to build, fully customized
C · 05

Compliance & Risk

BAA & Contract Pre-Review

The pain: Every vendor agreement needs compliance review. Outside counsel at $300–$500/hour makes thorough review cost-prohibitive. Things get signed without being read.

The prompt

"Review this Business Associate Agreement and identify: (1) missing provisions required under HIPAA 45 CFR 164.504(e), (2) provisions creating unusual risk for our org as the covered entity, (3) breach notification timeline compliance (HIPAA requires 60 days), (4) indemnification gaps to push back on, (5) 5 specific questions for legal counsel before signing. [Paste BAA text]"

What you get

  • Pre-legal review memo focused on real risk
  • Attorney time focused on reviewing, not reading
  • Legal cost per contract down 60–80%
  • Nothing gets signed without being understood

D · 01

Leadership & Strategy

Multi-Audience Strategic Communication

The pain: The same message needs to land differently for the board, for all-staff, for the community, and for department heads. Leaders write four versions. Or they write one and it lands wrong in three rooms.

The prompt

"We're consolidating two of our three emergency departments into a new regional emergency center — cost-driven but also improves clinical outcomes through volume concentration. Draft: (1) board memo — formal, financial/strategic framing, 1 page, (2) all-staff email — honest, empathetic, addresses job security directly, (3) community statement for media — accessible, patient care focused, (4) department head talking points for team conversations."

What you get

  • 4 audience-specific communications from one prompt
  • Consistent facts, differentiated tone
  • Job security addressed — not avoided
  • Ready for comms team review, not rewrite
Before: Half a day across 4 drafts → After: 20 min to draft all four
D · 02

Leadership & Strategy

Board & Leadership Meeting Prep

The pain: Board prep consumes a disproportionate amount of leadership time. The raw data exists — turning it into a clear narrative is the work.

The prompt

"Board meeting in 48 hours. Using this Q1 financial summary [paste data], create: (1) 5-bullet executive summary — what's working, what isn't, why, (2) 3 questions the board is likely to ask + my recommended responses, (3) a one-page narrative I can walk through in 10 minutes, (4) the single most important decision I need from the board today, framed as a clear decision question."

What you get

  • Full board meeting prep package in under 20 min
  • Anticipated questions with prepared answers
  • A clear decision question — not a status report
  • Narrative that tells a story, not just recites data
The shift: Leaders spend less time producing the package and more time thinking about what they actually want the board to decide.
D · 03

Leadership & Strategy

Competitive Intelligence & Market Positioning

The pain: Strategic decisions get made on intuition because real market research takes time no one has. The competitive landscape changes faster than the last presentation gets updated.

The prompt

"Research the competitive landscape for outpatient behavioral health services in the Atlanta, GA metro area. I want: (1) top 5 providers by market presence and differentiators, (2) recent market entries, closures, or expansions in the past 12 months, (3) most common gaps patients report in this market, (4) where a new entrant with strong clinical quality and tech-enabled care management could differentiate. Synthesize into a 1-page competitive brief."
Use: Perplexity for current, cited market data

What you get

  • 1-page competitive brief with sources
  • Current market intelligence — not 18-month-old analyst reports
  • Board-ready strategic framing
  • Positioning gaps identified
Verify: Click the sources. Competitive intelligence is only as good as the underlying data. Perplexity's source links make verification fast.
D · 04

Leadership & Strategy

EHR Implementation Change Communication

The pain: EHR implementations fail because of people, not technology. The communication plan exists as a slide deck that no one executes consistently.

The prompt

"We're implementing a new EHR over 18 months with 800 clinical and administrative staff. Create a change communication plan: (1) phased communication calendar from announcement through post-go-live with recommended channels per phase, (2) all-staff announcement — honest about the difficulty, clear about support, (3) manager FAQ with the 10 hardest questions managers will get, (4) 30-day post go-live pulse check email to surface problems early."

What you get

  • 18-month communication backbone
  • Manager toolkit — the hardest questions, pre-answered
  • Pulse check built in from the start
  • Change communication that actually gets executed
Real talk: The communication plan that doesn't get written is the one that causes the $3M EHR implementation to fail at go-live. This takes 20 minutes to draft.
D · 05

Leadership & Strategy

Strategic Decision Framing

The pain: Leaders have the data and the instinct. The missing piece is a structure rigorous enough to align a leadership team or board.

The prompt

"I'm considering acquiring a 3-location urgent care chain in our market. Help me think through this rigorously. Create: (1) structured decision framework — key strategic questions to answer before proceeding, (2) acquisition vs. organic growth vs. partnership pro/con analysis, (3) top 5 due diligence questions, (4) most common failure reasons for this type of acquisition + how I'd mitigate each, (5) one-page decision memo for my board."

What you get

  • A structured, rigorous decision framework
  • Options analysis — not just the one you're leaning toward
  • Failure mode awareness before commitment
  • Board-ready decision memo
The real value: AI as a thinking partner surfaces the questions you didn't know to ask — before the decision is made, not after.
