## PRODUCT POSITIONING

This is not a business training. This is a personal toolkit.

The business modules (M1–M4) are about what AI can do for your organization. This is about what AI can do for *you* — your time, your career, your finances, your family, your decisions. Every example is drawn from real life, not the workplace.

**Who it's for:** Any adult who wants to use AI tools to live smarter, work faster, and make better decisions — regardless of industry, role, or technical background.

**What it delivers:**
- Immediate confidence with AI tools (removes the "I don't know where to start" barrier)
- 15+ concrete use cases they can run today
- A personal AI habit that sticks

**Voice:** Warm, direct, peer-level. Like a smart friend who figured this out and is showing you how.

## PART 1: PERSONAL PRODUCTIVITY

### The Core Idea
Most adults spend 2–4 hours a day on tasks that are mostly writing, researching, or organizing information. Almost none of that is work that requires their unique judgment. AI handles the production so your brain can handle the thinking.

### Use Case P1: The Weekly Brain Dump
**The pain:** Sunday anxiety — the week ahead feels unmanageable. Tasks scattered across your head, your phone, three apps, and a sticky note.
**What AI does:** Takes your messy list of tasks and thoughts and turns it into a structured weekly plan with priorities, time blocks, and a clear first move.

**Prompt:**
```
Here's everything on my plate this week — it's messy, but here's the brain dump: [paste everything]. Help me turn this into a structured weekly plan. Group tasks by type (urgent, important-not-urgent, delegatable). Suggest which days to tackle which clusters. Flag anything that's likely to take longer than I think. Give me one clear "start here" task for Monday morning.
```
**What you get:** A structured week plan in 3 minutes. The Sunday anxiety dissolves when you can see the week laid out with an actual starting point.

---

### Use Case P2: Email That Writes Itself
**The pain:** Staring at a blank reply for 20 minutes. The email isn't complicated — but getting the tone right, saying everything you need to say, and not sounding annoying takes mental energy you don't have.
**What AI does:** Draft any email in your voice — from a difficult response to a landlord to a thank-you to someone who did you a favor.

**Prompt:**
```
Write an email for me. Context: [explain the situation]. Recipient: [who they are and your relationship]. What I need to communicate: [key points]. Tone: [polite but firm / warm and personal / professional but friendly]. Keep it concise — I hate long emails.
```
**Follow-up:** "Make it shorter" or "Make it warmer" or "Add that I'm available to discuss by phone"
**What you get:** First draft in 30 seconds. Usually needs one small edit. Sent in 3 minutes instead of 20.

---

### Use Case P3: Research Without the Rabbit Hole
**The pain:** You need to research something — a new appliance, a medical symptom, a neighborhood to move to, a car repair — and you go online and 90 minutes later you're 15 tabs deep and more confused than when you started.
**What AI does:** Synthesize research into a clear, structured summary that answers your actual question.

**Prompt (with Perplexity for current info):**
```
I need to research [topic]. My specific question is: [what you actually want to know]. Give me: (1) the key facts I need to understand this topic, (2) the 2–3 things most people get wrong or miss, (3) what I should do or decide next based on this information. Keep it practical, not encyclopedic.
```
**What you get:** Focused, structured answers to your actual question — not 47 results to sort through yourself.

---

### Use Case P4: The Hard Conversation — Prepared
**The pain:** You have a conversation you're dreading — asking for a raise, talking to a difficult family member, having a performance discussion, confronting a friend. You spend days anxious about it instead of ready for it.
**What AI does:** Help you prepare: what to say, how to say it, what they might say back, and how to respond.

**Prompt:**
```
I have a difficult conversation coming up and I want to prepare. The situation: [explain]. The person: [who they are, your relationship, their likely reaction]. What I want to accomplish: [your goal]. Help me: (1) draft what I want to open with, (2) anticipate the 3 most likely pushback responses and how I'd answer each, (3) identify the one thing I should avoid saying. Then offer to role-play the conversation with me.
```
**What you get:** You walk into the conversation prepared, not just worried.

---

### Use Case P5: Summarize Anything
**The pain:** Long documents, terms and conditions, meeting transcripts, academic articles, long email threads — things you technically need to read but don't have time to read carefully.
**What AI does:** Summarize any document into exactly what you need to know.

**Prompt:**
```
Summarize the following [document / email thread / article] for me. I want: (1) the key points in plain language, (2) anything that requires my attention or decision, (3) any red flags or things that seem unusual. [Paste document]
```
**What you get:** A 5-minute read becomes a 45-second read. You stay informed without drowning in content.

## PART 2: CAREER ADVANCEMENT

### The Core Idea
Your career is a product. AI helps you market it, develop it, and navigate it — faster and more strategically than doing it alone.

### Use Case C1: Resume That Gets Read
**The pain:** Your resume is fine. But "fine" doesn't get callbacks. It needs to be tailored to each job, keyword-optimized, and written in the language hiring managers actually respond to.
**What AI does:** Rewrite and tailor your resume for any specific role.

**Prompt:**
```
Here is my current resume: [paste resume]. Here is the job description I'm applying for: [paste JD]. Rewrite my resume to: (1) match the language and keywords in this job description, (2) quantify my accomplishments wherever possible — ask me for numbers if you need them, (3) lead with my strongest relevant experience for this specific role. Keep it to one page if possible.
```
**What you get:** A tailored, optimized resume for each application in 10 minutes. Most people spend 2–3 hours on this or send the same resume everywhere.

---

### Use Case C2: Interview Prep That Actually Works
**The pain:** "Tell me about yourself" and you freeze. Or you ramble. Or you answer the question they asked but not the question they meant.
**What AI does:** Build a complete interview prep kit and run mock interviews with you.

**Prompt:**
```
I have an interview for [role] at [type of company/organization]. My background is [brief summary]. Help me prepare: (1) craft a strong 90-second "tell me about yourself" answer tailored to this role, (2) generate the 8 most likely interview questions for this type of role, (3) help me build a STAR-format answer for 3 behavioral questions using my actual experience. Then offer to conduct a mock interview with me.
```
**What you get:** You walk into the interview with a structure, not just hope.

---

### Use Case C3: Negotiating What You're Worth
**The pain:** Most people don't negotiate. Or they do it poorly — either too timid or too aggressive — because they don't have a script.
**What AI does:** Research market rates, build your negotiation case, and give you the words to say.

**Prompt (use Perplexity first for market data):**
```
I'm preparing to negotiate my salary for [role] in [city/region]. My current offer is [amount]. I have [X] years of experience and [key skills/credentials]. Research: (1) the current market range for this role in this area, (2) the top factors that justify the higher end of the range. Then help me write: (3) the exact email or script I'll use to counter, (4) my response if they push back or say the offer is firm.
```
**What you get:** Data-backed negotiation with words you can actually use. Most people who negotiate get 5–15% more. Most people don't negotiate at all.

---

### Use Case C4: Build Your Personal Brand
**The pain:** You know you should be more visible online — LinkedIn, a professional bio, a clear point of view in your field — but you don't know what to say or how to say it without sounding like you're bragging.
**What AI does:** Help you articulate who you are, what you stand for, and write content that builds your professional presence.

**Prompt:**
```
Help me build my professional brand. Here's who I am: [describe your role, industry, experience]. Here's what I'm most passionate about professionally: [your interests/values]. Here's what I want to be known for: [your goal]. Help me: (1) write a strong LinkedIn headline and summary, (2) draft 3 LinkedIn posts on topics I care about — give me 3 options with different angles, (3) write a 3-sentence professional bio I can use anywhere.
```
**What you get:** A professional presence that actually sounds like you — without the cringe of writing about yourself.

---

### Use Case C5: Learn Anything Faster
**The pain:** You want to learn something new — a skill, a software, a subject area — but you don't know where to start and formal courses are either too slow, too expensive, or not quite right for what you need.
**What AI does:** Act as your personal tutor, curriculum designer, and practice partner for any subject.

**Prompt:**
```
I want to learn [topic/skill]. My current level is [complete beginner / I know the basics / intermediate]. My goal is [what you want to be able to do]. I have about [X hours/week] to dedicate to this. Design a learning plan for me: what to learn first, in what order, what resources to use, and how to practice. Then teach me the first concept right now.
```
**What you get:** A personalized learning curriculum and immediate first lesson — in one conversation.

## PART 3: LIFE & BIG DECISIONS

### The Core Idea
The biggest decisions in life — home, health, money, legal — are the ones people are least equipped to navigate. AI doesn't replace a doctor, lawyer, or financial advisor. But it makes you a much better-informed client who asks better questions, understands the answers, and doesn't pay for an expert to explain basic concepts.

### Use Case L1: Understand Any Legal Document
**The pain:** Terms and conditions, lease agreements, contracts, insurance policies — documents you're supposed to sign that you don't actually understand. Most people sign them anyway. Some of them matter.
**What AI does:** Translate legal language into plain English and flag anything unusual or risky.

**Prompt:**
```
Explain this document to me in plain language. I need to understand: (1) what I'm agreeing to in simple terms, (2) any obligations I'm taking on that could cost me money, time, or rights, (3) anything that seems unusual compared to a standard [lease / contract / policy], (4) the 3 most important things I should know before signing. [Paste document]
```
**Important note to always include:** AI gives you a plain-language summary, not legal advice. If something looks risky, talk to a lawyer. But you'll walk in knowing exactly what to ask.

---

### Use Case L2: Health Research You Can Actually Use
**The pain:** You or a family member gets a diagnosis, a test result, or a medication prescribed — and you leave the appointment with more questions than when you arrived. Or you go online and scare yourself with worst-case scenarios.
**What AI does:** Explain medical information in plain language, help you understand your options, and prepare you with better questions for your next appointment.

**Prompt:**
```
Help me understand [diagnosis / medication / test result / procedure]. Explain: (1) what this actually means in plain language, (2) the most common treatment approaches and what the evidence says, (3) questions I should ask my doctor at my next appointment, (4) things I should watch out for or tell my doctor right away. Please be specific but clear — I'm not a medical professional.
```
**Important note:** AI doesn't replace your doctor. It helps you have a better conversation with them.

---

### Use Case L3: Home Buying & Renting — Navigate the Process
**The pain:** Buying or renting a home involves more unfamiliar terms, processes, and potential mistakes than almost any other transaction in adult life. Most people figure it out by making expensive errors.
**What AI does:** Explain every step, decode every document, and help you ask the right questions at every stage.

**Prompt:**
```
I'm [buying / renting] a home and I have questions about [the inspection process / what's in my lease / what closing costs I can negotiate / what questions to ask the landlord / how to evaluate a neighborhood]. Give me: (1) a plain-language explanation of how this works, (2) the 5 most important things I should know that most people miss, (3) specific questions I should ask [the realtor / landlord / inspector / lender] before moving forward.
```

---

### Use Case L4: Personal Finance — Understand and Plan
**The pain:** Financial planning feels overwhelming — budgets, retirement accounts, insurance, debt — and professional advice is expensive. Most people avoid it until it's an emergency.
**What AI does:** Help you understand financial concepts, evaluate your options, and build a clear picture of where you stand and what to do next.

**Prompt:**
```
Help me think through my personal finances. My situation: [brief description — income range, main expenses, any debt, savings goal]. I want to understand: (1) how to prioritize between [paying down debt / building an emergency fund / saving for retirement / saving for a goal], (2) what I should know about [specific financial topic — 401k, Roth IRA, credit score, insurance], (3) a simple action plan for the next 30 days that doesn't require a financial advisor. Keep it practical and specific to my situation.
```
**Important note:** AI gives you a framework. A certified financial planner gives you personalized advice. AI makes the planner conversation more productive.

---

### Use Case L5: Parenting & Family — Hard Questions Made Easier
**The pain:** Parenting throws questions at you constantly — how to talk to your kid about something hard, how to navigate a school situation, how to handle a family conflict. There's rarely a clear right answer and usually no time to research.
**What AI does:** Help you think through difficult family situations, prepare for hard conversations with children, and find resources when you need them.

**Prompt:**
```
I need help navigating a family situation. [Describe the situation — child's age, what happened, what you're trying to accomplish]. Help me: (1) understand what's going on from my child's / family member's perspective, (2) figure out the right approach for this situation and this relationship, (3) give me specific language I can actually use — how to start the conversation, what to say if it goes sideways. Tone: practical and warm, not clinical.
```

## CURRICULUM STRUCTURE

### Session Flow (2 hours, self-paced or facilitated)
- **Opening (10 min):** Why AI is the most useful tool most people aren't using for their own life
- **Part 1 — Productivity (40 min):** 5 use cases, 2 live demos
- **Part 2 — Career (40 min):** 5 use cases, 2 live demos
- **Part 3 — Life Decisions (30 min):** 5 use cases, key guardrails
- **Close (10 min):** Build your personal AI toolkit — what are you starting with today?

### The Three Guardrails (taught throughout)
1. **AI drafts, you decide** — especially for health, legal, financial matters
2. **The more specific you are, the more useful it is** — vague prompts get vague answers
3. **Verify anything important** — especially numbers, dates, and medical/legal specifics
